Our Commitment to Sustainability

Coconut Palm Wood

Coconut trees begin bearing fruit within 4 years and produce for around 60 years. When plantations cut down these trees, much of the palm wood is burned as waste. We rescue this sustainable material to create beautiful utensils, reducing waste and supporting circular economy practices.

Reclaimed Ebony Wood

Our dark ebony utensils are made from reclaimed off-cuts from ebony wooden furniture craftsmen. By using these materials that would otherwise be discarded, we reduce waste and give new life to beautiful wood that deserves to be appreciated.

Handcrafted Quality

Each utensil is individually cut, sanded, and cleaned by skilled artisans. A virgin coconut oil polish is applied to enhance the natural beauty and protect the wood. Every piece is unique, just like you!

Care & Maintenance

Cleaning

  • Hand wash with mild soap and warm water
  • Use a soft cloth or sponge to clean
  • Avoid harsh scrubbers or abrasive materials
  • Rinse thoroughly and dry immediately
  • Do not soak for extended periods

Maintenance

  • Occasionally apply food-safe oil (mineral or coconut oil)
  • This maintains shine and prevents cracking
  • Apply oil every 2-3 months or as needed
  • Wipe excess oil with a soft cloth
  • Store in a cool, dry place

Important Notes

  • Not recommended for dishwasher use
  • Avoid prolonged soaking in water
  • Do not expose to extreme heat
  • Keep away from direct sunlight for long periods
  • With proper care, utensils will last for years

Storage Tips

  • Store in the included canvas pouch for protection
  • Keep in a cool, dry location
  • Avoid storing in humid environments
  • Use the pouch for travel and on-the-go dining
  • Display proudly in your kitchen or dining area
