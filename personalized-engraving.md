## 💡 Pro Tips

- Test your design print at 3″ size for detail visibility

- Use contrast checker tools for optimal engraving depth

- For text: Minimum 8pt font (Sans Serif font family recommended)

## Laser Engraving

The surface of the coconut wood is melted and vaporized by the heat generated by the laser beam by a process known as “material removal”. The recess formed on the surface of the coconut wood becomes the engraving.

## 🔍 Design Preparation

- ✏️ Convert all text to outlined paths

- 🖼️ Remove background elements

- 🔳 Use solid fills instead of gradients

- 📏 Include 1:1 scale reference in file

To get started, consider the theme or concept you’d like to incorporate into your design. Whether it’s a nature-inspired motif, a meaningful quote, or a personalized monogram, the design options are limitless. You can also consider the size and placement of your design on the coconut bowl, as well as any additional elements such as patterns or borders.

## 🎨 Design Requirements

- **File Format**: Submit vector files (SVG, AI, EPS, PDF) for crisp results. Raster images (PNG/JPG) require **300+ DPI resolution**.

- **Dimensions**:

- Max engraving area: [15cm \times 8cm]

- Account for bowl curvature – allow 10% margin

- **Line Thickness**: Minimum 0.5pt (0.18mm) for visibility

- **Color Mode**: Black/white only – laser interprets black as engraving zones

Once you have a clear idea of the design you’d like to create, it’s time to bring it to life.
