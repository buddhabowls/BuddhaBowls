Coconut Bowl Care Guide

Keep your coconut bowls beautiful and functional for years to come

Learn how to properly care for your natural coconut bowls

Quick Care Tips

Hand Wash Only

Always hand wash with warm water and mild soap. Never use the dishwasher.

Avoid Extreme Heat

Don't microwave or expose to direct heat. Keep away from hot stoves and ovens.

Oil Regularly

Apply food-grade coconut oil monthly to maintain shine and prevent cracking.

Store Properly

Keep in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.

Cleaning Your Coconut Bowl

1

Rinse Immediately

After use, rinse your bowl with warm water immediately. This prevents food from drying and sticking to the surface. Use a soft cloth or sponge to gently wipe away any residue.

2

Hand Wash with Mild Soap

Use a soft sponge or cloth with warm water and mild dish soap. Gently scrub the interior and exterior surfaces. Avoid abrasive scrubbers that can damage the natural finish.

3

Rinse Thoroughly

Rinse completely with clean water to remove all soap residue. Make sure no soap remains on the bowl, as it can affect the taste of your food.

4

Dry Immediately

Pat dry with a soft cloth immediately after washing. Don't let your bowl air dry, as prolonged moisture can cause the wood to swell or warp. Store only when completely dry.

5

Oil Monthly

Once a month, apply a thin layer of food-grade coconut oil or mineral oil to the entire bowl. Rub it in with a soft cloth and buff away any excess. This keeps the bowl hydrated and beautiful.

How to Oil Your Coconut Bowl

Regular oiling is essential to keep your coconut bowl hydrated, beautiful, and protected. Here's how to do it properly:

Choose the Right Oil

Use food-grade coconut oil, mineral oil, or beeswax. Avoid vegetable oils that can go rancid. Our recommended oil is food-grade coconut oil, which is natural and safe.

Apply a Thin Layer

Use a soft cloth to apply a thin, even layer of oil to the entire bowl. Don't oversaturate—a little goes a long way. Focus on the interior and exterior surfaces.

Buff and Polish

Use a clean, dry cloth to buff away any excess oil. Rub in circular motions to distribute the oil evenly and bring out the natural shine of the coconut shell.

Let It Set

Allow the oil to set for a few hours before using the bowl. This ensures the oil is fully absorbed and provides maximum protection and shine.

Pro Tip: Oil Selection

We recommend food-grade coconut oil because it's natural, food-safe, and won't go rancid like other vegetable oils. It also has a pleasant aroma and provides excellent protection for your bowl.

Removing Stains & Odors

Turmeric or Curry Stains

Solution: Mix baking soda with a little water to form a paste. Gently rub onto the stained area with a soft cloth. Let sit for 15 minutes, then rinse thoroughly. Repeat if necessary.

General Discoloration

Solution: Cut a lemon in half and rub it directly on the stained area. The natural acidity helps lighten stains. Rinse well and dry immediately. Follow with an oil treatment.

Coffee or Tea Stains

Solution: Soak a cloth in white vinegar and place it on the stain for 10-15 minutes. Gently scrub with a soft brush, rinse thoroughly, and dry immediately.

Lingering Odors

Solution: Sprinkle baking soda inside the bowl and let sit overnight. Rinse thoroughly in the morning. Alternatively, place a bowl of baking soda inside overnight to absorb odors.

Water Rings or Marks

Solution: Rub with a cloth dampened in white vinegar, then dry immediately. Apply a thin layer of food-grade oil to restore the finish and prevent future water marks.

Mold or Mildew

Solution: Mix equal parts white vinegar and water. Wipe the affected area thoroughly and dry immediately. Ensure proper storage in a dry location to prevent recurrence.

Proper Storage

Temperature Control

Store your coconut bowls in a cool, dry place away from direct heat sources. Extreme temperature fluctuations can cause the natural material to crack or warp. Ideal temperature range is 60-75°F (15-24°C).

Avoid Direct Sunlight

Keep your bowls away from direct sunlight, which can fade the natural color and cause the material to dry out. Store in a cabinet, pantry, or covered shelf for best results.

Humidity Control

Maintain moderate humidity levels. Too much moisture can cause mold or mildew, while too little can cause cracking. Use a dehumidifier if your storage area is particularly damp.

Protective Placement

Store bowls upright or stacked carefully with padding between them. Avoid stacking too high to prevent damage. Keep away from areas where they might be knocked over or dropped.

Away from Chemicals

Store away from cleaning chemicals, pesticides, and other harsh substances. These can damage the natural finish and potentially contaminate your food bowls.

Regular Inspection

Check your stored bowls periodically for signs of damage, mold, or pest activity. Ensure they remain in good condition and address any issues immediately.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

My bowl has small cracks. Is it still safe to use?

Small surface cracks are normal in natural coconut shells and don't affect usability. However, deep cracks that go through the bowl may cause leaking. If you notice deep cracks, stop using the bowl and contact our support team for a replacement.

Why is my bowl becoming lighter in color?

Natural coconut shells lighten over time with exposure to light and air. This is completely normal and doesn't affect the bowl's functionality. To slow this process, store in a dark place and apply oil regularly to maintain the color.

Can I use my bowl for hot foods or beverages?

We recommend using coconut bowls for room-temperature or cold foods and beverages only. Hot liquids can damage the natural material and potentially cause the finish to deteriorate. Let hot foods cool before serving in your coconut bowl.

My bowl feels rough or splintery. What should I do?

Gently sand the rough area with fine-grit sandpaper (220+ grit) in the direction of the grain. Sand lightly to avoid damaging the bowl. After sanding, apply food-grade oil to restore the finish and smoothness.

Is my bowl food-safe?

Yes! All our coconut bowls are 100% food-safe. They're finished with non-toxic, food-grade treatments and are naturally antimicrobial. The coconut material itself is completely safe for food contact.

Can I use my bowl for acidic foods like citrus or vinegar?

Yes, but use caution. Acidic foods won't damage the bowl, but prolonged contact may affect the finish. Rinse and dry immediately after use. Avoid leaving acidic foods sitting in the bowl for extended periods.

How long will my coconut bowl last?

With proper care, your coconut bowl can last for many years—even decades! Regular cleaning, oiling, and proper storage are key to longevity. Many of our customers have bowls that are 5-10+ years old and still in great condition.

What if my bowl develops a bad smell?

Soak a cloth in white vinegar and wipe the bowl thoroughly. Let it air dry completely. Alternatively, place baking soda inside overnight to absorb odors. Ensure the bowl is completely dry before storage to prevent future odor issues.
