Seasonal Treats That Pair Perfectly with Cinnamon

To truly embrace the magic of cinnamon, why not pair its scent with some delicious seasonal treats? Here are a few simple recipes that complement the cozy vibes of cinnamon candles and essential oil.

#### 1. **Cinnamon Sugar Donuts** These fluffy, sweet treats are perfect for any occasion. Serve them warm with a cup of spiced tea or coffee for an indulgent pairing. **Ingredients:** - 1 cup all-purpose flour - 1/2 cup sugar - 1 tsp baking powder - 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon - 1/4 tsp salt - 1/2 cup milk - 1 egg - 2 tbsp melted butter - Cinnamon sugar (for coating) **Instructions:** 1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a donut pan. 2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt. 3. Add milk, egg, and melted butter, mixing until smooth. 4. Pour the batter into the donut pan and bake for 10–12 minutes or until golden brown. 5. Let cool slightly before coating each donut in cinnamon sugar.

#### 2. **Spiced Apple Cider** Nothing says “cozy” like a warm mug of spiced apple cider infused with cinnamon sticks. **Ingredients:** - 6 cups apple cider - 3 cinnamon sticks - 4 whole cloves - 2 star anise pods (optional) - 1 orange (sliced) - Honey or brown sugar (to taste) **Instructions:** 1. In a large pot, combine all ingredients and bring to a simmer over medium heat. 2. Reduce heat to low and let the cider simmer for 20–30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. 3. Strain before serving and garnish with a cinnamon stick or orange slice.

#### 3. **Cinnamon-Spiced Chocolate Bark** This easy-to-make treat is perfect for gifting or snacking during gatherings. **Ingredients:** - 12 oz dark chocolate (chopped) - 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon - 1/4 tsp nutmeg (optional) - Chopped nuts or dried fruit for topping **Instructions:** 1. Melt the chocolate in a microwave-safe bowl or over a double boiler. 2. Stir in ground cinnamon (and nutmeg if desired). 3. Pour the melted chocolate onto a parchment-lined baking sheet and spread evenly. 4. Sprinkle chopped nuts or dried fruit on top and let it cool until hardened. 5. Break into pieces and serve or package as gifts.
