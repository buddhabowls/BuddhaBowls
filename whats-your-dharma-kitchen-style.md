## What’s Your Dharma Kitchen Style?

Welcome to the ultimate culinary personality quiz—the one that reveals not just who you are in the kitchen, but who you are *in life*. Are you the kind of person who meticulously dices onions into perfect 1/8-inch cubes, or do you throw in whole garlic cloves and call it rustic? Do you plate your food like an Instagram influencer, or are you just trying to make sure it’s edible? Let’s dive into the five Dharma Kitchen Styles and see where you fit on the chopping board of life.

Cooking—some call it an art, others call it a daily battle against hunger and questionable expiration dates. Whether you’re a culinary Picasso or someone who just discovered that water boils faster with the lid on, your cooking habits, food preferences, and kitchen organization say a lot about you. Let’s dive into five hilariously revealing questions that might just make you laugh at your own kitchen quirks.

## What’s Your Dharma Kitchen Style?

### **1. Do You Measure Ingredients or Just Channel the Spirit of Your Ancestors?**

Ah, the age-old debate: measuring cups or “eyeballing it”? Some cooks meticulously follow recipes as if they’re decoding a treasure map, while others pour spices into the pot with the confidence of someone who doesn’t own a fire extinguisher.

If you measure everything down to the last teaspoon, you’re probably the type who alphabetizes your spice rack and has a spreadsheet for meal planning. If you don’t measure? Well, you might be the person who says, “Oops, too much salt,” and then tries to fix it by adding more…everything else. Either way, your approach to measuring is a window into your soul—or at least into how often you set off your smoke alarm.

### **2. What’s Your Go-To Comfort Food?**

Your favorite comfort food is like your culinary spirit animal. Mac and cheese? You’re all about nostalgia and carbs, probably eating it straight out of the pot while watching reruns of your favorite sitcom. Sushi? You’re fancy and mysterious—or just really good at convincing yourself that raw fish counts as “light eating.”

And let’s not forget pizza. If pizza is your go-to, you’re likely someone who believes in solutions that come in circular, cheesy form. Bonus points if you’ve ever eaten cold pizza for breakfast while pretending it counts as “multitasking.”

### **3. How Do You Organize Your Kitchen (Or Do You)?**

There are two types of people in this world: those who organize their kitchen like it’s a Tetris championship and those who treat it like a scavenger hunt. Are your pots and pans neatly stacked, or is finding the right lid a 20-minute cardio workout?

Some people label everything, including their sugar and flour containers (we see you, Pinterest overachievers). Others have a “system” that only they understand—like keeping the olive oil next to the cereal because “it just makes sense.” Spoiler alert: it doesn’t make sense.

### **4. What’s Your Stance on Leftovers?**

Leftovers are either a blessing or a curse, depending on who you ask. Are you the person who proudly repurposes last night’s dinner into a gourmet lunch? Or do you shove those Tupperware containers into the back of the fridge like they’re in witness protection?

Let’s be honest: there’s always that one container of mystery leftovers that’s been in your fridge so long it’s practically family. You don’t throw it out because you’re afraid it might have developed sentience—and also because you’re holding onto hope that it’s still edible (it’s not).

### **5. What’s Your Signature Dish?**

Everyone has that one dish they make to impress people—or at least convince them that you know what you’re doing in the kitchen. Maybe it’s a perfectly roasted chicken or a batch of cookies that could win awards (or at least bribe friends).

But let’s not forget about the “signature dish” that happens accidentally—like when you burn toast so often that people start calling it your specialty. Hey, charcoal is trendy, right?

### **Conclusion: Your Kitchen, Your Rules**

Whether you’re a gourmet chef or someone who thinks toast is a challenge, your cooking habits tell a story—and it’s usually a funny one. So go ahead, embrace your quirks! Measure with precision or pour with reckless abandon. Organize like Martha Stewart or embrace the chaos. At the end of the day, as long as you’re fed and laughing at yourself along the way, you’re doing just fine.

Now excuse me while I go check on my “signature dish” (a.k.a. something I forgot was in the oven). Bon appétit!
