Almond Butter Berry Bliss Nice Cream

A refreshing blend of mixed berries and almond butter, perfect for summer

15 mins
Serves 2
Vegan
Healthy

Experience the burst of flavors with Almond Butter Berry Bliss Nice Cream. This isn't just a dessert; it's an experience. It's creamy, dreamy, and packed with enough natural goodness to make your inner health nut do a happy dance. Served in a coconut bowl, this treat is as Instagram-worthy as it is delicious.

Let's talk about dessert. Specifically, the kind of dessert that doesn't leave you clutching your stomach in regret or swearing off sugar for the next 48 hours. This sweet treat is so good, it'll make you question why you ever bothered with regular ice cream in the first place.

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe bananas
  • 2 tbsp almond butter
  • 1 cup mixed berries
  • 1 tsp honey (optional)

It's Customizable

Not into berries? Swap them out for mangoes or peaches. Hate almond butter? Try peanut butter or tahini. The world is your frozen dessert oyster.

It's Guilt-Free(ish)

Sure, it's still dessert, but it's the kind you can enjoy without that pesky side order of guilt. Win-win!

The Method to the Madness

Making this nice cream is so simple that calling it a "recipe" feels almost unfair. It's more of a life hack disguised as dessert. Here's how you do it:

1

Freeze Your Bananas

Peel those ripe bananas, slice them into chunks, and toss them in the freezer. This step is non-negotiable unless you want banana mush instead of nice cream (and trust me, you don't). Freeze overnight or for at least 4 hours.

2

Blend Like You Mean It

Once your bananas are frozen solid, throw them into a high-powered blender or food processor along with the almond butter, mixed berries, and honey. Blend until smooth and creamy. Your blender might sound like it's auditioning for a heavy metal band at first, but stay the course—it'll calm down soon enough.

3

Serve in Style

Scoop your masterpiece into a coconut bowl for maximum aesthetic points. If you don't have a coconut bowl, any bowl will do—but don't blame me if it doesn't feel as magical.

4

Optional Garnish Game

Feeling fancy? Top your nice cream with extra berries, a drizzle of almond butter, or even some shredded coconut. Go wild—it's your dessert kingdom.

Chef's Tips & Variations

Pro Tip: Banana Ripeness

Use bananas with brown spots for maximum sweetness and creaminess. The riper the banana, the better the nice cream.

Make It Ahead

Freeze the blended nice cream for 30 minutes for a firmer texture, or serve immediately for a soft-serve consistency.

Nut Butter Swap

Try peanut butter, cashew butter, or tahini for different flavor profiles. Each brings its own unique taste.

Berry Variations

Use frozen mango, peaches, or strawberries instead. You can also add a handful of spinach for a sneaky green boost.

Sweetener Options

Skip honey and use maple syrup, agave, or dates for a different sweetness. Or go unsweetened for a tart berry flavor.

Texture Control

Add a splash of almond milk if your blender struggles. A little liquid goes a long way in creating the perfect consistency.

Storage & Serving

Freezer Storage

Store leftover nice cream in an airtight container in the freezer for up to 2 weeks. Thaw for 5 minutes before serving if it's too hard.

Best Served

Serve immediately after blending for soft-serve consistency, or freeze for 30 minutes for a firmer scoop. Enjoy with a spoon in your favorite coconut bowl!

Prep Ahead

Freeze banana slices in advance for quick nice cream whenever the craving strikes. Just blend and enjoy!

Final Thoughts

Almond Butter Berry Bliss Nice Cream isn't just a recipe; it's a lifestyle choice—a declaration that you can have your dessert and feel good about it too. So go ahead, grab those bananas and dust off your blender. Your coconut bowl is waiting to be filled with creamy, berry-studded happiness.

And if anyone asks why you're eating dessert straight from a coconut shell at 10 a.m., just tell them you're living your best life. Because honestly? You are.
