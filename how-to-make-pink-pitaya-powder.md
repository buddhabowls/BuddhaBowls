**How to Make Pink Pitaya Powder:**

**Ingredients:
**– 2-3 ripe pitaya fruits
– Lemon juice (optional)

**Instructions:
**1. Start by cutting the pitaya fruits in half and scooping out the flesh with a spoon.
2. Spread the pitaya flesh evenly on a dehydrator tray, making sure it’s not too thick.
3. If you don’t have a dehydrator, you can also use an oven set to the lowest temperature. Place the pitaya flesh on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
4. If desired, drizzle some lemon juice over the pitaya flesh. This will help preserve the vibrant pink color.
5. Dehydrate the pitaya flesh for 12-24 hours, or until completely dried out. If using an oven, keep the door slightly ajar to allow moisture to escape.
6. Once dried, transfer the pitaya flesh to a [Blender](https://amzn.to/4oSJQHl) or spice grinder and process until it forms a fine powder.
7. Store the pink pitaya powder in an airtight container away from direct sunlight.

## How To Make Fruit Powder

Do you ever find yourself with an abundance of fresh fruit that you just can’t seem to eat before it goes bad? Or maybe you want to find a way to enjoy your favorite fruits year-round? Well, making fruit powder is a great solution! Fruit powder is not only a convenient way to enjoy the taste of your favorite fruits, but it also has a long shelf life and can be used in a variety of recipes. In this blog post, we will walk you through the simple steps of making fruit powder at home.

### Step 1: Choose Your Fruit

The first step in making fruit powder is to choose the fruit you want to use. Almost any fruit can be turned into powder, but some popular choices include berries, apples, bananas, and citrus fruits. Consider the flavor and sweetness of the fruit, as well as how easily it can be dried and ground into a powder.

### Step 2: Prepare the Fruit

Once you have chosen your fruit, it’s time to prepare it for drying. Start by washing the fruit thoroughly to remove any dirt or pesticides. Then, depending on the type of fruit, you may need to peel, core, or pit it. For example, with berries, you can simply remove any stems and leaves, while with apples, you may need to peel and core them.

### Step 3: Dry the Fruit

There are a few different methods you can use to dry the fruit. One option is to use a food dehydrator, which is designed specifically for drying fruits and other foods. If you don’t have a dehydrator, you can also dry the fruit in the oven on a low heat setting. Simply spread the prepared fruit out on a baking sheet and place it in the oven until it is completely dry. Another option is to air dry the fruit by placing it in a sunny spot or using a fan to circulate air.

### Step 4: Grind the Dried Fruit

Once the fruit is completely dry, it’s time to grind it into a powder. You can use a coffee grinder, food processor, or [Blender](https://amzn.to/4oSJQHl) to do this. Simply place the dried fruit in the grinder or processor and pulse it until it becomes a fine powder. Be sure to store the fruit powder in an airtight container to keep it fresh.

### Step 5: Enjoy Your Fruit Powder

Now that you have made your own fruit powder, there are so many ways to enjoy it! You can add it to smoothies, yogurt, oatmeal, or baked goods for a burst of natural fruit flavor. Fruit powder can also be used to make homemade fruit-flavored drinks, sauces, and even natural food coloring for icing and frosting. The possibilities are endless!
