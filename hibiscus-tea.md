Dried Hibiscus Uses

Dried hibiscus is a versatile ingredient that can be used in a variety of ways. Whether you’re looking to add a pop of color to your dishes or enjoy its unique flavor, there are plenty of ways to incorporate dried hibiscus into your diet.

One of the most popular uses for dried hibiscus is to make hibiscus tea. Simply steep a handful of dried hibiscus flowers in hot water for a few minutes, then strain and enjoy! You can also add other herbs or spices to customize the flavor to your liking.

Dried hibiscus can also be used to make a delicious hibiscus syrup, which can be drizzled over pancakes, waffles, or mixed into cocktails for a refreshing twist. To make hibiscus syrup, simply simmer dried hibiscus flowers with water and sugar until it forms a thick, flavorful syrup.

If you’re feeling adventurous, you can even use dried hibiscus to make hibiscus-infused oil or vinegar. Simply combine dried hibiscus flowers with your choice of oil or vinegar and let it infuse for a few weeks. The result is a unique and flavorful ingredient that can be used in salad dressings, marinades, or as a finishing touch for roasted vegetables.

Hibiscus Quinoa Salad in a Coconut Bowl

Ingredients:

– 1 cup cooked quinoa

– 1/4 cup dried hibiscus flowers

– 1/2 cup diced cucumber

– 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved

– 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint

– 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese

– 2 tbsp hibiscus syrup

– Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions:

1. In a small bowl, soak the dried hibiscus flowers in hot water for 10 minutes. Drain and set aside.

2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, cucumber, tomatoes, mint, and feta cheese.

3. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the hibiscus syrup with salt and pepper.

4. Pour the hibiscus syrup over the quinoa salad and toss to combine.

5. Spoon the quinoa salad into a coconut bowl and garnish with the soaked hibiscus flowers.
Coconut Hibiscus Popsicles

Ingredients:

– 1/2 cup dried hibiscus flowers

– 2 cups water

– 1/4 cup [Honey](https://amzn.to/44rhrke) or agave nectar

– 1 can coconut milk
Instructions:

1. In a small saucepan, bring the dried hibiscus flowers and water to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.

2. Strain the hibiscus-infused water into a bowl and stir in the [Honey](https://amzn.to/44rhrke) or agave nectar until dissolved.

3. In a separate bowl, whisk the coconut milk until smooth.

4. Pour the hibiscus water into popsicle molds, filling each mold about halfway.

5. Gently pour the coconut milk over the hibiscus water in each mold until full.

6. Insert popsicle sticks and freeze for at least 4 hours before serving.
