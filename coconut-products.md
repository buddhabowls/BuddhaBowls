## Tree of Life

		Every bit of the coconut is used in our coconut products. 
As a result, coconuts are called the “Tree of Life” and can produce drink, fiber, food, fuel, [Coconut Bowl Utensils](https://buddhabowls.org/product/coconut-bowl-utensils/), musical instruments, and much more.		

## Sustainable Resource

		Most of the world’s coconuts are grown in small, independent farms in the Philippines and Indonesia. 
Certified organic farming methods greatly reduce the amount of pesticides, herbicides, fertilizers and fungicides applied to crops. 
Coconut trees are a significant and very valuable resource, with such a wide variety of uses, and they are fully sustainable when managed properly.		

## Renewable Resource

		Sustainable resources are classified as natural resources that are renewable and can be replenished at the same rate, or faster than they are being consumed. 
Coconut farming is extremely regenerative as one tree alone can yield over 300 coconuts per month. 
For a natural resource to be sustainable, it must be: Renewable. Managed responsibly.
