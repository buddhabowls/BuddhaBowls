ingredients:
  - Containers or pots
  - Healthy riverbank grape vine
  - Plastic bags or a humidity dome
  - Potting soil
  - Rooting hormone
  - Sharp pruning shears
  - water
instructions:
  - The best time to take cuttings is in late winter or early spring when the vine is still dormant.
  - Look for healthy, disease-free vines. Select stems that are at least one year old and have a diameter of about 1/4 inch.
  - Using sharp pruning shears, cut 6-8 inch sections of the vine. Ensure that each cutting has at least 2-3 buds.
  - Remove any leaves from the lower half of the cuttings. This helps reduce moisture loss and the risk of rot.
  - Dip the cut end of each cutting in rooting hormone to encourage root development. This step can improve success rates but is not strictly necessary.
  - Fill your containers with potting soil. Make a hole in the soil and insert the cutting about 2-3 inches deep, ensuring at least one bud is below the soil surface. Space the cuttings about 4-6 inches apart if using a larger container.
  - Water the cuttings thoroughly after planting. The soil should be moist but not soggy.
  - Cover the containers with plastic bags or place them in a humidity dome to maintain moisture levels. Ensure the bags are not touching the cuttings directly.
  - Place the containers in a bright location, but avoid direct sunlight which can overheat the cuttings. A spot with indirect light is ideal.
  - Check the soil regularly to ensure it stays moist. Remove the plastic covering occasionally to allow for air circulation.
  - After 4-8 weeks, gently tug on the cuttings to check for resistance, which indicates root development.
  - Once the cuttings have a strong root system, transplant them into larger pots or directly into the garden. Choose a sunny location with well-draining soil.
