**Take this fun quiz to discover your brushing style!**

Are you a gentle brusher or an aggressive scrubber? Find out what type of brusher you are with this entertaining quiz!

When it comes to oral hygiene, the way you brush your teeth can say a lot about your personality. Are you a gentle brusher, taking your time and being mindful of your teeth and gums? Or are you an aggressive scrubber, attacking your teeth with vigor and determination? Take this entertaining quiz to find out what type of brusher you are!

## What Type of Brusher Are You?

**Question 1: How long do you typically spend brushing your teeth?**

a) Less than a minute

b) 1-2 minutes

c) 2-3 minutes

d) More than 3 minutes
**Question 2: How much pressure do you apply when brushing your teeth?**

a) Very light pressure

b) Moderate pressure

c) Firm pressure

d) I press as hard as I can
**Question 3: How often do you replace your toothbrush?**

a) Once a year or less

b) Every 3-4 months

c) Every 2-3 months

d) Every month or less
**Question 4: Do you use a soft, medium, or hard bristle toothbrush?**

a) Soft bristle

b) Medium bristle

c) Hard bristle
**Question 5: How do you feel after brushing your teeth?**

a) My teeth feel clean and refreshed

b) My gums feel sore and irritated

c) I don’t notice any difference

d) I feel like I’ve scrubbed away all the plaque and bacteria
Now, let’s tally up your answers and see what type of brusher you are!

**If you answered mostly a’s:**

Congratulations, you are a gentle brusher! You take a mindful and gentle approach to brushing your teeth, focusing on thoroughness rather than aggressiveness. You understand the importance of being gentle on your teeth and gums to prevent damage and irritation.
**If you answered mostly b’s:**

You fall into the category of a moderate brusher. You strike a balance between being gentle and thorough, ensuring that you effectively clean your teeth without causing harm to your gums. Keep up the good work, but be mindful not to apply too much pressure.
**If you answered mostly c’s:**

You are an assertive brusher. You tend to apply firm pressure when brushing your teeth, which can lead to gum irritation and enamel wear if not careful. Consider adjusting your technique to be gentler on your teeth and gums.
**If you answered mostly d’s:**

You are an aggressive scrubber! You approach brushing with vigor and determination, but this can lead to overbrushing, gum recession, and enamel erosion. It’s important to dial back the intensity and focus on being gentle yet effective in your brushing technique.
No matter what type of brusher you are, it’s important to be mindful of your brushing technique and its impact on your oral health. Remember to use a soft-bristled toothbrush, replace it regularly, and brush for at least two minutes twice a day. And don’t forget to schedule regular dental check-ups to ensure that your oral hygiene routine is keeping your teeth and gums healthy.

So, what type of brusher are you? Whether you’re a gentle brusher or an aggressive scrubber, there’s always room for improvement in taking care of your oral health.

Keep brushing, but do it with care!
